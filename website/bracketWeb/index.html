About Bracket

<

bracket

>

is an app that offers fill-in-the-blank coding challenges. For every correct answer, the player will be rewarded 1 point. A leaderboard displays who has the top 10 highest scores. The player may choose from the 5 categories: Recursion, Data Structures, Algorithms, Time Complexity, and Loops.

This is a great tool for interview practice, general understanding, competition, and fun!

The app includes interactive completion of challenges. The styling and coloring is similar to that of Sublime Text.

Instructions For Using

<

bracket

>

  1. Login

    To start, if you already have an account, click login. Otherwise, click the register button as seen below and complete all necessary information. From there, you will be redirected to the Choose A Challenge page. If you have logged in within the last few days, the application will automatically keep you logged in and Choose A Challenge will be the first page to show up.

    Login iOS

    Register iOS

    Login Android

    Register Android

  2. Choose A Challenge

    Next, if you'd like to start completing challenges, you should choose one of the categories, which are computer science concepts. The five options are: Recursion, Data Structures, Algorithms, Time Complexity, and Loops. This page can also be accessed via the 'Challenges' tab.

    Choose A Challenge iOS

    Choose A Challenge Android

  3. Challenge Scroll-View

    The page you will be shown after choosing a category contains a swipe view of the challenges for that topic. You can scroll left and right through them to see the title and description of each one. You may also use the next and previous buttons to do this. Once you find one you like, click it.

    Scroll View iOS

    Scroll View Android

  4. Challenge

    Once on the challenge page, you will notice that there is a comment at the top with a more detailed description of the challenge and a snippet of code that lies below it. Within the snippet, there are little white text boxes. Those must be filled in with the correct values to make the code work. To check if you are correct, click the submit button. Any text boxes that have a red background are incorrect, and any text boxes that have a green background are correct. Capitalization and whitespace are not taken into account when responses are checked. Each correct box rewards you with one point. The total points are equivalent to the number of white boxes. Your scores for each challenge will be recorded. However, the application will only keep your highest score for each challenge.

    Challenge Not Complete iOS

    Challenge Complete iOS

    Challenge Not Complete Android

    Challenge Complete Android

  5. User

    To see information regarding your account, number of challenges completed/attempted, and total points, you should go to the profile page. This can be reached by clicking on the button in the tab view that says 'Profile'.

    Profile iOS

    Profile Android

  6. Leaderboard

    There is a ranking of the top 10 highest scores under the tab that says 'Leaderboard'. It shows the username and number of points for each ranking.

    Leaderboard Android
